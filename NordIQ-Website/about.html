The Story

I'm Craig Giannelli. I've spent 20 years building infrastructure systems that don't fail—from financial trading platforms to high-frequency analytics engines. I've been paged at 3 AM more times than I can count. I've seen $100K outages happen because monitoring tools said everything was "green" until it wasn't.

NordIQ exists because I was tired of reactive monitoring. Tired of emergency responses. Tired of incidents that could have been prevented with the right intelligence. Traditional monitoring tells you when things are broken. I wanted something that tells you they're about to break—with enough time to do something about it.

In 2025, I started a project: build a predictive monitoring system using state-of-the-art AI. Not another threshold-based alerting tool—a true predictive intelligence system that sees problems before they happen.

I built NordIQ in 158 hours.

That's not a typo. Using Claude Code as my amplifier, I compressed what would've been 1-2 years of solo development into focused sprints of deep work.

But this isn't just AI magic. It's 20 years of domain expertise crystallized into algorithms that understand infrastructure the way an SRE does. It's contextual intelligence, not binary thresholds. It's profile-aware risk scoring, not one-size-fits-all alerts.

NordIQ is lean engineering at its finest: no bloat, no committees, no death by meeting. Just precision, speed, and a relentless focus on preventing incidents before they impact users.

The Saturday Ritual

"I use Claude Code far more than I'd like to admit—but I'm not complaining. It's allowed me to build something genuinely exceptional and push far beyond what I initially thought possible."

5:00 AM - Coffee & Context

I brew coffee, boot up VS Code, and spend a moment catching Claude back up. Since Claude Code has no memory between sessions, we've developed a protocol: end each day with a deliberate handoff.

I maintain what we call our "RAG document"—really just session context—where we capture where we left off, what we're thinking, and what comes next. Claude reads it like a brief before a sprint.

The Codex of Truth

Context alone isn't enough. Projects drift. So we keep a "Codex of Truth"—a living document of rules, constraints, and decisions.

When we hit a feature freeze, the Codex keeps us both honest. Instead of scope creep, we document ideas and chart the roadmap. Adding explainable AI to the dashboard? That was a late addition, after we lifted the freeze, because we'd already committed it to the roadmap together.

11:00 AM - Shipped

By 11 AM, we've shipped: cleaned-up codebase, updated documentation, rebuilt features. That's not humanly possible alone. That's also not possible if Claude's flying blind between sessions.

The real magic isn't Claude; it's the dialogue. We keep each other accountable. We catch each other's mistakes. When I forget to wrap a session properly, Claude explores the codebase, finds the gaps, and stitches everything back together. That's partnership.
